Honorable Speaker of the House of Representatives,
Honorable Chairperson of the National Assembly,

**1.** It is with great pride that I rise in this honorable House to continue the tradition — initiated about 75 years ago (on 21 Magh 2008 B.S.) by Shri Suvarna Shamsher, the first Finance Minister of modern Nepal — of presenting the country's statement of revenue and expenditure. The objective of that time, "a better standard of living for the people," remains just as relevant today. On this occasion I pay heartfelt tribute to the martyrs who sacrificed their lives in the various movements for civil liberty, democratic rights, social justice and a prosperous Nepal, and I express my solidarity with the citizens injured in those movements.

**2.** The awakening of a change-seeking youth force that rose in favor of accountable governance, integrity and civic dignity, and the vast popular mandate obtained on the new political ground thus established, have entrusted the present government with extraordinary responsibility. As Finance Minister, I have accepted this not merely as a constitutional formality but as a duty to transform the character of the state, the culture of governance and the very form of the economy. I am confident that this budget will become a credible policy instrument that meets the legitimate expectations of all Nepalis at home and abroad for a production-oriented economy, international prestige and technology-friendly change.

**3.** With the generational transition in Nepal's politics, there is an ardent anticipation of transformation across every dimension of national life. Embracing the favorable conditions brought by irreversible change, we are committed to establishing standards of good governance through refinement in policy, law, institutions, systems, processes, leadership and intent. Ending policy drift, procrastination, institutional capture and the plunder of state resources — and building a results-oriented system of governance — is the need of the hour. Although the present constitution has laid an important foundation for breaking the cycle of social and cultural exclusion, deeper structural reform is needed for equitable access to economic opportunities.

**4.** The time has come to grapple with the challenges and opportunities created by the specialized diversification and limitless expansion of information and communication and artificial intelligence, of finance, trade and investment; by the collective strength of the Nepali community spread across the world; and by the aggressive adoption of technology. The responsibility of building a virtuous cycle of equitable economic growth rests on our shoulders. On this journey, on behalf of the government, I make a heartfelt request to the Federal Parliament, all organs of the state, civil society and all the Nepali people for constructive suggestions and cooperation.

**5.** The indicators of the country's current economic condition, the obstacles to progress, and possible paths to their solution have been made public in the "Current Economic Situation Paper, 2083." The principles and priorities of next fiscal year's policies and programs and of the Appropriation Bill have already shed light on the government's substantive policy direction. Likewise, the detailed statistical account — the "Economic Survey, 2082/83" — has already been tabled in Parliament.

**6.** The country now stands at a decisive juncture of sweeping economic reform. The vicious cycle of political instability has ended. Most macroeconomic indicators are improving. The financial system has ample liquidity along with robust foreign-exchange reserves; there is vast potential in clean-energy production and new technology; and the youth demographic stands as a great form of capital. Nevertheless, instability in international supply chains, sluggishness in the domestic market, low investment in the productive sector, and distrust toward public institutions remain as challenges. The aim of this budget is to transform these challenges into opportunities and to simultaneously raise the state's service-delivery capacity, the private sector's confidence, and citizens' trust.

Honorable Speaker, Honorable Chairperson — at the outset, I announce some notable reforms related to the foundation of the all-round development that this FY 2083/84 budget seeks to establish.

**7.** To provide relief to enterprises and businesses, expand the middle class, and energize the overall economy, I have undertaken a broad review of tax rates.

**(a)** To reduce the tax burden, I have doubled the income-tax exemption threshold, raising it to NPR 1 million (ten lakh) for individuals. I have reduced the maximum rate of personal income tax by ten percentage points.

I have reduced customs duty on 273 types of raw materials so that the duty on industrial raw materials is at least one tier below that on finished goods. I have limited the existing eleven-tier customs-duty structure to seven tiers.

I have abolished the excise duty currently levied on 360 goods. I have consolidated scattered taxes collected at the customs point — such as the infrastructure-development tax and the road repair and improvement charge — into the green tax.

I have provided that Section 57 of the Income Tax Act, 2058, shall not apply to involuntary disposal upon the death of a natural person, nor to a change of ownership in a controlled entity arising from such a change of ownership. *(This clause is partly garbled in the source OCR.)*

I have arranged that the capital gains tax levied on the sale of listed companies' securities shall be final.

We will implement a provision under which consumers making purchases through digital payment receive a 10 percent discount on value added tax at the moment the invoice is issued. We will automate the VAT refund system. To promote the culture of issuing and taking invoices in the purchase and sale of goods and services, attractive programs such as a lottery will be launched.

We will form a high-level committee to study and submit recommendations on the relevance of multiple VAT rates.

Adopting a policy of making tax compliance a partner in development rather than a punishment, I have introduced a special scheme on tax exemption and relief — to resolve long-standing tax disputes created by legal ambiguity and taxpayer ignorance, and to create a business-friendly environment.

To resolve tax disputes pending before courts or judicial bodies — whether brought by the government or the taxpayer — I have arranged that if the taxpayer pays the assessed tax amount plus one percent within the prescribed deadline and withdraws the case, the fees, fines, additional charges, late fees, and interest shall be waived.

**8.** The Government of Nepal's existing cumbersome organizational structure will now be made lean. Given that the number of federal ministries has already been fixed at 18 (down from 22), I have adopted a policy of using the funds saved by cutting departments, offices, and positions to make the administration efficient. We will abolish 31 bodies, merge 6, transfer 6, and restructure 18. With the aim of making the bureaucracy capable of a dignified livelihood, high-performing, efficient, and accountable, arrangements have been made for the continuous and respectable increase of the service benefits of civil, military, police, and teacher personnel.

**9.** Cost reduction and efficiency enhancement will be another dimension of institutional reform. To this end, we will control recurrent expenditure while maintaining unprecedented frugality and allocative efficiency. I have substantially reduced office operating costs. I have removed allocations for training expenses outside training centers and for equipment such as cameras and televisions. We will end the practice of creating various funds to provide perks. I have removed the discounts given to officials and staff of public enterprises on the purchase of goods and services produced by those enterprises. Until the adjustment of positions and management of liabilities of merged and abolished bodies is completed, I have frozen the amounts allocated to those bodies. These steps are preliminarily estimated to save about NPR 20 billion.

**10.** Appropriate policy and legal arrangements will be made for investment promotion, economic reform, and smooth service delivery. For this, we will draft, replace, or amend dozens of acts, rules, procedures, and directives. To immediately repeal the 15 laws already announced, we will present a Bill to Amend Certain Nepal Acts in Parliament.

**(a)** By amending company law, we will bring clarity on matters such as conflict of interest and disclosure, and ease the company-liquidation process. We will conclude foreign-investment protection and double-taxation-avoidance agreements with additional countries. By amending the Insolvency Act, 2063, we will help resolve the financial problems of consumers and of micro, small, and medium enterprises. To promote investment, we will draft a limited liability partnership law, which will encourage angel investment to flow into venture capital and private equity funds.

We will ease the arrangements allowing Nepali citizens to invest abroad. By amending the Industrial Enterprises Act, we will make it sufficient merely to notify the Department of Industry on matters such as capacity expansion, change of ownership, and capital increase. By amending the Foreign Investment and Technology Transfer Act, we will provide that prior approval of Nepal Rastra Bank is not required for the repatriation of investment and that mere notification suffices. We will bring convertible instruments, project-tied funding, and other hybrid instruments within the scope of foreign investment. We will remove the prior-approval requirement within the automatic-approval process for foreign investment. We will simplify the process of remitting service fees, royalties, and technology payments abroad. We will make legal provisions for the recovery of bad debts, and will soon draft a law on intellectual-property protection. We will arrange that credit can be extended based on a creditworthiness assessment of individuals and businesses derived from analysis of their past economic activity. We will establish a separate tribunal for the swift resolution of commercial disputes, and reform the Mediation Act.

To enable non-resident Nepalis to participate in the secondary securities market, we will amend the legal provisions on foreign-investment approval, accounting of investment, profit repatriation, and capital gains tax.

By amending the National Civil Code, 2074, we will provide that foreign investors in Nepal, international organizations, and branches of multinational companies may take on long-term lease — for residential purposes — buildings in the residential sector (not exceeding 25 percent of total units) or apartments at locations specified by the government.

**(f)** To ensure financial access for small and medium entrepreneurs, we will guarantee loans on a "first-loss recovery" basis. *(Partly garbled in the source OCR.)*

**11.** Committing to launch an aggressive series of operational reforms and to change the pace and rhythm of capital expenditure, we will work in "mission mode" — improving the procurement process, ensuring flexibility in resource management (including reallocation), mobilizing alternative finance, and ensuring the stability of project chiefs — to complete projects at the prescribed cost and on time. A sunset law on development projects will be presented in Parliament within the current fiscal year. We will arrange tracking so that mobilization-advance funds can be spent only on the relevant project. A preliminary pipeline of infrastructure projects that can be built under the hybrid-annuity model will be ready within three months. We will suitably address the difficulties faced by construction businesses due to the rise in the price of fuel, bitumen, and other construction materials caused by the Middle East conflict.

**12.** Clean cities, easy transport, and safe roads are integral parts of a dynamic economy. We will develop roads, transport, drinking water, sanitation, and economic-access infrastructure in an integrated manner. Within the next five years, the roads and bridges connecting all district headquarters will be upgraded. By improving travel safety and road standards, we will minimize the human losses caused by accidents.

**13.** The government will be vigilant in establishing harmony — not conflict — between development and the environment. To this end, the Environment Protection Act, 2076, and the Forest Act, 2076, will be amended. We will provide that a supplementary environmental impact assessment is not required where the forest area or the number of trees to be felled is less than stated in the approved EIA report. We will significantly reduce the multi-stage chain of decisions extending up to forest-area approval and tree-felling orders.

**14.** The government's fiscal pressure will be reduced through new and alternative financial instruments. In this connection, I have proposed new financial instruments to mobilize capital from outside the government's balance sheet and to bring in public investment and technology. The law on the Alternative Development Finance Fund — to mobilize private investment in infrastructure — has already been passed by the House of Representatives. A Nepali-currency offshore bond will soon be issued in the international market. I have adopted a policy of issuing clean-energy bonds and diaspora bonds and of making maximum use of available climate funds.

**15.** Given the currently comfortable level of foreign-exchange reserves, we have adopted a policy of mobilizing a certain percentage as a "sovereign wealth fund." We will establish a "Matribhumi (Motherland) Fund" to invest in strategically important projects such as three months' fuel storage and an AI factory. To reduce the exchange risk on projects funded by foreign loans or investment, I have arranged to provide hedging services at an appropriate premium from the coming fiscal year. We will create a favorable economic environment to improve the results of the first and second sovereign credit ratings. We will remove Nepal from the money-laundering "grey list" as quickly as possible.

**16.** To strengthen public enterprises, I have arranged capital increases in some and divestment of government shares in others. I have proposed increasing the Government of Nepal's capital in Rastriya Banijya Bank. We will convert Nepal Airlines Corporation into a company and identify an appropriate method for management improvement and strategic partnership. We will issue shares of the Rastriya Life Insurance Company and Bishal Bazar Company to the general public. We will merge Hydroelectricity Investment and Development Company Limited with a financial institution of matching objectives to create a specialized infrastructure-sector entity. Within the coming year, we will complete due-diligence (asset and liability) assessment of seven public enterprises, including Gorakhkali Rubber Industry, and arrange investment under a public-private partnership model.

**17.** The government will keep its keen focus on expanding information-technology infrastructure. Retaining the federal government's 66 percent share in Nepal Telecom, we will sell the remaining shares to the public by the end of Poush and use the proceeds to make Nepal a "tech hub." To expand the IT service sector, I have arranged to open up investment abroad. We will make clear legal arrangements to allow work from Nepal for foreign employers and to attract remote work. We will arrange for the IT software used by government bodies to be procured through a single agency. We will increase investment in digital public infrastructure. We will establish a fintech marketplace under the supervision of Nepal Rastra Bank. Dozens of government services will be integrated into the Nagarik App.

**18.** We have begun a special initiative to usher Nepal confidently into the age of AI (artificial intelligence). We will establish the country's first "Sovereign AI Compute Center" at Syuchatar, Kathmandu. We will purchase thousands of AI processing units and provide subsidized compute capacity to AI entrepreneurs and start-ups. I have proposed converting clean hydroelectric energy into high-value AI compute services. I believe that harnessing Nepal's vast energy potential for the development of artificial intelligence will build a foundation for skilled employment, digital exports, and AI-based economic transformation. In the coming fiscal year, we will grant prestigious fellowships to at least fifteen Nepali researchers who have built international reputations in the field of AI, inviting them to return and contribute. We will give high priority to academic disciplines, such as mathematics, that will play an important role in the age of AI.

**19.** The long-pending restructuring of the Nepal Electricity Authority will be completed. We will split the NEA into three separate companies — for generation, for transmission and distribution, and for trade. We will cancel the licenses of projects that signed power purchase agreements but did not begin construction, and arrange to make new PPAs on a "take-or-pay" basis. We will immediately conclude PPAs for electricity projects below ten megawatts of capacity. We will arrange to set a reduced electricity purchase rate during the dry season and conclude purchase agreements accordingly.

We will arrange for the private sector to trade electricity in the international market. We will make legal provisions enabling the building of transmission lines and the trading of electricity on payment of a wheeling charge, including the necessary permissions. When ownership of a hydropower project transfers to the Government of Nepal after the permit period, we will arrange to adjust the fees for forest-land usufruct and tree felling. To encourage the private sector to develop large and reservoir-type projects, we will adopt a policy that, where the promoter ensures one-hundred-percent investment in a reservoir-type project, up to forty percent of shares may be sold to the general public in the first year.

**20.** We will increase investment in the all-round provincial development of rural and peri-urban areas that have excellent potential for economic growth. To develop such areas as "growth poles and quads," we will implement an integrated plan of policy and physical infrastructure.

**(a)** Based on the 122,000-hectare command area to be irrigated by the Sunkoshi–Marin Diversion, and by completing the remaining work on the Hulaki and East–West highway expansions, a mid-Madhesh agriculture-and-industry quadrangle will be created.

**(b)** Along the "Karnali Quadrangle" — the Mid-Hill (Pushpalal) Highway, the Karnali Highway, the Bheri Corridor, and the route from Rara via Jumla to Phoksundo — arrangements will be made to foster herbs, hydropower, tourism, and mining activities.

**(c)** We will swiftly complete the Butwal–Narayangadh and Mugling–Damauli–Pokhara sections of the East–West Highway. Beginning the international-standard transformation of the Siddhartha Highway, we will make the origin area of the Kaligandaki civilization a pilgrimage-tourism and clean, high-value-business-centered "Gandaki corridor." Integrating Triveni Dham, Devghat, Ruru Kshetra (Ridi), Kagbeni, Muktinath, and Damodar Kunda, we will develop the "Shaligram Path."

**(d)** Promoting the "Nirvana Path" based on the Lumbini–Muktinath religious-cultural route, we will integrate meditation, sadhana, yoga, Buddhist–Vedic philosophy, and the Himalayan cultural experience of Manang, Mustang, and Myagdi. We aim to connect the Mid-Hill Highway with northern eco-trade destinations such as Olangchunggola, Kimathanka, Tatopani, Rasuwagadhi, Korla, and Hilsa.

**(e)** We will advance the Koshi Corridor plan, covering the region from Koshi Tappu to Kanchenjunga, centered on biodiversity conservation and clean-tourism development.

**(f)** We will advance the development of a distinctive "tourism quad" in the Far West, covering the Ramaroshan, Khaptad, Badimalika, Shaileshwari, Ugratara, and Mallikarjun areas.

**21.** To implement the long-term plan of "Vision Kathmandu, 2040," we will build integrated infrastructure for the overall urban-service management of the Kathmandu Valley — including underpasses, flyovers, intersection improvements, river-corridor infrastructure, park construction, solid-waste management, utility ducts, and the undergrounding of electrical wires. In cooperation with provincial and local governments, we will take special initiatives, including financial incentives, for the protection and expansion of open spaces. We will launch a smart urban mobility program in the Kathmandu Valley with electric public buses, charging stations, a tracking system, smart bus parks, and strong institutional arrangements. In the Pokhara Valley too, we will soon launch e-mobility services to replace public buses with electric buses.

We will make intensive preparations to make Visit Nepal Year 2085 and Nepal Arogya (Wellness) Year 2027 successful. I have arranged physical and financial incentives for the construction and operation of high-value, tourist-targeted resorts and hotels. Tourism activities will be branded under the core theme of "wellness tourism," and the source markets for tourists will be diversified. To remove Nepal from the European Union's "Air Safety List," we will restructure the Civil Aviation Authority by next Poush and establish separate roles for the regulator and the operator.

**22.** The revival of the agriculture sector is among the government's top priorities. We will strengthen the chain of agricultural production, processing, and marketing, and improve the entire ecosystem of distribution and marketing for farmers. Adopting a policy of attracting commercial agriculture and encouraging productivity growth, we will transform agriculture into a dignified and profitable enterprise.

**(a)** I have prepared a pilot program under which the government provides up to a 40 percent incentive grant to farmers making an initial capital investment of up to NPR 20 million in agricultural and livestock production. Such a grant will be reimbursed at an annual rate of 10 percent for four consecutive years from the date production begins. Providing genuine farmers with support in improved seeds, fertilizer, irrigation, electricity, and agricultural insurance, we will prevent the misuse of agricultural subsidies. Providing up to an 80 percent premium grant on agricultural and livestock insurance, I have set a direction to phase out other subsidies gradually.

I have arranged resources to complete large and under-construction irrigation projects within this fiscal year. To expand small and medium irrigation projects across the country, we will establish a challenge fund with Government of Nepal investment and have local governments compete for it.

**(c)** I have adopted a policy of providing concessions — such as subsidized loans and interest subsidies — for purchasing agricultural produce directly from farmers. We will provide production and branding support to industries established in remote areas that process organic, high-value, and geographical-indication (GI) goods.

We will restructure the agricultural service system into a "farmer-centered service system." To expand farmers' access to agricultural services at their doorstep — year-round technical services, digital agricultural information, and private service providers — we will deploy the necessary technical workforce in every local government.

**(e)** Reviving agricultural research and development centers across the country, I have prioritized research focused on improved-seed development, varieties suited to local climates, agro-biological technology, and productivity growth.

**(f)** To provide legal arrangements for ensuring fair prices for agricultural produce, contracts between farmers and buyers, subsidies, and regulation, we will soon present an Agriculture Bill in Parliament.

**23.** Taking forests and natural resources beyond the limited scope of conservation, we will make them a basis for green industrialization, employment creation, and import substitution. We will promote sustainable timber production, forest-based industries, the processing of herbs and non-timber forest products, and green enterprises. To enhance the competitiveness of the domestic timber industry, we will review the sale price of raw timber collected during development and construction work. We will fully digitize the permit system for the felling, collection, transport, and distribution of forest products into a single-window system, removing procedural hassles. We will grant community forest user groups greater autonomy in production, processing, marketing, and enterprise operation. We will remove the inconsistencies among the laws and policies relating to forests, environment, industry, tourism, and infrastructure. We will economically value the carbon stock, ecosystem services, and natural capital of forest areas and incorporate them into the budget and planning system.

**24.** To implement the "Investment Express" concept, we will introduce an automatic-route system within the next three months. Integrating all services — from company and industry registration, financial services, and tax-system participation to visa applications — we will arrange for information access, compliance reporting, and risk-based inspection. We will provide that projects approved by the Investment Board do not require re-approval from other bodies. We will provide an investment visa for investment-related study and research on approved projects. By simplifying share transfer, tax clearance, asset valuation, royalty payment, dividend distribution, and the liquidation process, we will ease investment and profit repatriation.

**25.** The government will be firmly committed to industry and industrial innovation. In line with the policy of expanding quality industrial infrastructure, industrial areas such as Motipur and Mayurdhap will be built and operated by the private sector. In the Panchkhal Special Economic Zone, we will give priority to women entrepreneurs. We will provide additional concessions to women entrepreneurs in credit flow. We will arrange a "Special Economic Administration Zone" in which all decisions on tax, customs, import, export, and investment for the special economic zone are made from a single point.

**(a)** To enhance the competitiveness of manufacturing industries, we will review the electricity demand charge and provide concessions on electricity tariffs. Industrialists will be able to use structures built on land in industrial areas, special economic zones, and leased land as collateral for banking purposes.

**(b)** We will provide "business revival loans" to industries and businesses that have been unable to operate at full capacity due to a lack of capital, encouraging them to expand and grow.
