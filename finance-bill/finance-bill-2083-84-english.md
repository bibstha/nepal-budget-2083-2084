## Objectives and Reasons

This Bill has been presented with the objective of implementing the financial proposals of the Government of Nepal — by levying certain duties, taxes, fees and charges, continuing the prevailing rates, making necessary alterations, and amending certain prevailing Nepal laws relating to revenue administration.

This Bill includes provisions to fix revenue, taxes, fees, duties and charges, to increase or decrease their rates, or to grant partial or full exemptions. It also incorporates provisions relating to excise duty, value added tax, health risk tax, education service fee, education and health equity fee, domestic-production promotion fee, clean-infrastructure investment fee, foreign-employment service fee, road construction charge, road repair and improvement charge, film development fee, pollution control fee, telephone ownership fee, telecommunications service charge, casino royalty, electronic service tax, skill promotion and development fee, and green tax.

This Bill also proposes timely amendments to the Liquor Act, 2031; the Value Added Tax Act, 2052; the Income Tax Act, 2058; the Excise Duty Act, 2058; the Customs Duty Act, 2081; and the Customs Act, 2082.

This Bill has been presented to the Federal Parliament as a Money Bill (Finance Bill).

*(Dr. Swarnim Wagle)*
*Minister of Finance*

## The Act

**Preamble:** Whereas it is expedient to collect revenue, to reduce or increase taxes, to grant exemptions, and to amend the prevailing laws relating to revenue administration in order to implement the financial proposals of the Government of Nepal for the Fiscal Year 2083/84;

Now, therefore, the Federal Parliament has enacted this Act.

**1. Short title and commencement:** (1) This Act shall be called the "Finance Act, 2083." (2) Sections of this Act shall come into force as specified therein; the remainder shall come into force from the 1st of Shrawan, 2083 B.S. *(The exact list of section numbers in this sub-section is illegible in the source OCR.)*

**2. Value Added Tax:** Value added tax shall be levied and collected in accordance with the Value Added Tax Act, 2052.

**3. Excise Duty:** Excise duty shall be levied and collected, in accordance with the Excise Duty Act, 2058, on goods or materials produced or manufactured in factories within Nepal, on imported goods or materials, and on services.

**4. Customs Duty:** Customs duty shall be levied and collected, in accordance with the Customs Duty Act, 2081, on goods exported from Nepal and imported into Nepal.

**5. Foreign Employment Service Fee:** (1) In accordance with the prevailing federal law, a person licensed to operate a foreign-employment business shall deposit a foreign-employment service fee at one percent of the amount collected from a person going for foreign employment; such amount shall be spent for the welfare of the person going for foreign employment. (2) The licensed person shall deposit the fee under sub-section (1) into the revenue account by the 25th of the following month and send the details thereof to the Department of Foreign Employment. (3) If the fee is not deposited within that period, it shall be recovered with interest at fifteen percent per annum. (4) The administration of this fee shall be as determined by the Department of Foreign Employment.

**6. Pollution Control Fee:** (1) A pollution control fee shall be levied and collected at one rupee fifty paisa per liter on petrol and diesel sold and distributed within Nepal. (2) The fee shall be collected from the importer and deposited into the revenue account by the 25th of the following month. (3) If not deposited within that period, it shall be recovered from the importer with interest at fifteen percent per annum. (4) The administration of this fee shall be as determined by the Inland Revenue Department.

**7. Telephone Ownership Fee:** (1) A telephone ownership fee of five hundred rupees shall be collected from a customer installing a telephone; it shall also be collected from a person acquiring ownership when ownership of such telephone changes. Provided that, for mobile telephones, a telephone ownership fee of two percent shall apply on each SIM card and recharge. (2) The fee shall be collected by the telecom-service-providing entity and deposited into the revenue account by the 25th of the following month. (3) If not deposited within that period, it shall be recovered from the entity with interest at fifteen percent per annum. (4) The administration of this fee shall be as determined by the Nepal Telecommunications Authority.

**8. Telecommunications Service Charge:** (1) An entity providing telecom services such as telephone, mobile, and internet shall collect a telecommunications service charge of ten percent of the fee charged to customers. (2) Notwithstanding sub-section (1), such charge shall not apply to the interconnection charge payable between licensed telecom providers for using one another's networks, nor to up to fifty percent of the maintenance charge on fixed-broadband service fees. (3) The charge shall be collected by the licensed provider and deposited into the revenue account by the 25th of the following month. (4) If not deposited within that period, it shall be recovered with interest at five percent per month. (5)–(6) The administration and other procedures of this charge shall be as determined by the Nepal Telecommunications Authority.

**9. Green Tax:** A green tax shall be levied and collected at the customs point, as per Annex 1, on goods imported into Nepal.

**10. Internal Production Protection and Promotion Fee:** An internal production protection and promotion fee shall be levied and collected at the customs point, as per Annex 2, on goods imported into Nepal.

**11. Clean Infrastructure Investment Fee:** (1) A clean-infrastructure investment fee shall be levied and collected, as per Annex 3, on electric vehicles and transport equipment imported into or produced within Nepal. (2) For vehicles imported after this Act comes into force, the customs office shall collect the fee at the customs point; for those imported earlier but pending registration, and those produced domestically and coming into use, the registering office shall collect it from the producer at cost price at the time of registration. (3) The fee shall be deposited into the Federal Consolidated Fund. (4) The details of the fee collected shall be sent monthly to the Government of Nepal, Ministry of Finance.

**12. Road Construction Fee:** (1) A road construction fee shall be levied and collected, as per Annex 4, on vehicles and transport equipment imported into or produced within Nepal. (2) For vehicles imported after this Act comes into force, the customs office shall collect it at the customs point; for those imported earlier but pending registration, and those produced domestically and coming into use, the registering office shall collect it at the time of registration. (3) The fee shall be deposited into the Federal Consolidated Fund. (4) The details collected shall be sent monthly to the Government of Nepal, Ministry of Finance.

**13. Luxury Fee:** (1) A luxury fee shall be levied and collected, as per Annex 5, on goods and services consumed in Nepal. (2) The administration of the luxury fee shall be as determined by the Inland Revenue Department.

**14. Skill Promotion Fee:** (1) A skill promotion fee shall be levied and collected, as per Annex 6, on the sale of gold, silver, and their ornaments and articles to consumers in Nepal. (2) The administration of this fee shall be as determined by the Inland Revenue Department.

**15. Education Service Fee:** (1) When providing foreign-currency exchange for tuition to students going abroad to study, an education service fee of three percent shall be levied and collected on the amount of such exchange.

(2) The bank or financial institution providing the exchange facility shall deposit the collected education service fee into the revenue account by the 25th of the following month and report the details to the relevant revenue office. (3) If not deposited within that period, it shall be recovered with interest at fifteen percent per annum. (4) If a student who paid the fee does not get to go abroad and returns the foreign currency requesting a refund, it shall be refunded through the relevant bank per the procedure prescribed by the Inland Revenue Department. (5) The administration of this fee shall be as determined by the Inland Revenue Department.

**16. Education Equity Fee:** An education equity fee shall be levied and collected, as per Annex 7, on all kinds of fees charged to students by privately operated educational institutions. Such fee shall be spent on access to quality education and on building educational infrastructure.

**17. Health Equity Fee:** A health equity fee shall be levied and collected, as per Annex 8, on all kinds of service fees charged to patients by privately operated health-service providers. Such fee shall be spent on access to quality health services and on health-related infrastructure.

**18. Power to reduce, increase, or exempt rates:** (1) Notwithstanding anything in prevailing law, the Government of Nepal may, as necessary, reduce or increase the rate of any charge, fee, duty, or tax imposed under this Act or other prevailing law, or grant partial or full exemption from it. (2) Likewise, the Ministry of Finance may reduce the rate of, or grant partial or full exemption from, such charges/fees/duties/taxes for non-profit public and community institutions, the Government of Nepal, provincial governments, local levels, or organized institutions owned by the Government of Nepal. (3) The relevant ministry may — subject to the conditions in the agreement and to Annex 9 — grant partial or full exemption on goods imported in the name of the following projects or their appointed contractors: **(a)** projects operated with foreign loan or grant assistance; or **(b)** projects under an agreement with the Government of Nepal or the Investment Board Nepal. (4) If anyone misuses such an exemption, or uses goods obtained under an exemption for purposes other than the specified one (except where approved by the Government of Nepal), the exempted amount shall be recovered together with a fine equal to that amount. (5) The Ministry of Finance shall annually present to the Federal Parliament the details of rates reduced or increased and exemptions granted under this section. (6) Notice of such reductions, increases, or exemptions shall be published in the Nepal Gazette.

**19. Power to simplify procedures or remove obstacles:** In the course of implementing this Act, if a serious disturbance to public peace and order, an obstruction to public movement, or a disaster creates a situation in which a facility provided by this Act cannot be enjoyed, or if any complexity or obstacle arises in implementation, the Ministry of Finance may, by notice in the Nepal Gazette, extend deadlines, simplify procedures, or remove obstacles as necessary.

**20. Health Risk Tax:** (1) A health risk tax shall be levied and collected, as per Annex 10, on goods imported from abroad and produced in Nepal. (2) The administration of this tax shall be as determined by the Inland Revenue Department.

**21. Film Development Fee:** A film development fee shall be levied and collected, as per Annex 11, on the entry fee of foreign films screened by cinema halls in Nepal.

**22. Casino Royalty:** (1) A person or entity licensed to operate a casino in Nepal shall pay an annual royalty of NPR 55 million for operating a casino, and NPR 30 million annually for games played only through modern machines or equipment. (2) Under one license, such games may be operated at only one location. (3) The licensee shall deposit the royalty with the Department of Tourism — forty percent by the end of Poush, seventy percent by the end of Chaitra, and the balance by the end of Ashadh. (4) A licensee that fails to deposit within those periods shall pay an additional fee of fifteen percent of the royalty for up to three months past each deadline, and thereafter an annual additional fee of thirty percent on the remaining royalty. (5) The administration of the casino royalty shall be as determined by the Department of Tourism.

**23. Electronic Service Tax (Digital Service Tax):** (1) An electronic service tax shall be levied and collected at two percent on the transaction value of electronic services provided by a non-resident person to users located in Nepal. However, it shall not apply to: **(a)** annual transactions up to NPR 3 million; **(b)** sales made through a digital interface to business users in Nepal for business purposes. (2) A non-resident person providing electronic services in Nepal shall file the details of its transactions for each fiscal year, and the tax thereon, electronically as determined by the Inland Revenue Department, within three months of the fiscal year's end. (3) If the details are not filed within the prescribed period, a fee of 0.1 percent of the annual transaction amount per year shall apply; if the tax is not paid by the prescribed date, interest at fifteen percent per annum shall apply; and if the tax is understated or concealed, a fine of fifty percent of the understated or concealed amount shall also apply. (4) Income on which tax has been paid under this section shall not be subject to tax under the Income Tax Act, 2058.
